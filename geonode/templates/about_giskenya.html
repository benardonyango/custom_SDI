{% trans "A GIS-Kenya Project" %}

The KALRO GIS portal is an initiative of the GIS-Kenya Project funded by the McKnight Foundation through its Collaborative Crop Research Programme (CCRP).
The project’s long-term aim is a community of researchers that are applying spatial thinking throughout the scientific method for better research.
We hope to achieve this change through:

  1. Building capacity of researchers in KALRO,CCRP and universities in applying open source GIS tools and techniques in agricultural research
  2. Bridging the gap between theory and practice for university students through targeted but competitive industrial attachments and internships within the KALRO GIS laboratory
  3. Enhancing existing capacity of the GIS lab of KALRO, both human and infrastructure

Our collaborating partners are mainly Kenyan public universities, which include, but not limited to:

  1. The University of Nairobi - UoN
  2. Dedan Kimathi University of Technology - DeKUT
  3. Jomo Kenyatta University of Agriculture and Technology - JKUAT
  4. Technical University of Kenya - TUK
  5. The University of Eldoret - UoEld
